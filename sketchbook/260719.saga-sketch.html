Saga Sketch

Began with an exercise using two “Saga” stage sequencers on the OXI ONE with two closely-related sequences routed to two different voices. The primary voice is the K-Accumulator. The secondary voice, which doesn’t show up until ~1 minute in, is an E370. The initial recording is a performance of sorts, including the transpositions in the middle and end.

The bridge, which was created and inserted later, is the K-A with the same sequence, but dramatically slowed down, and then repeated two octaves lower.

Primary modulation of the K-A is a Planar joystick, mainly controlling the MORPH, but some onboard modulation is also employed. The E370 is simply sequenced through different wavetable positions. Probability-based velocity is modulating the envelope levels for both voices.

Samarkanda, patched as a ping-pong delay is featured. Some weird Swells reverb for the low pass gate strikes (derived from the secondary voice). Various Valhalla reverbs also employed on the DAW.

The percussion is just two channels of the Assimil8or using rotating samples. I had to re-record the percussion a couple times, simplifying it. The kick is operating in the “time signature” of the secondary voice, the snare is operating in the “time signature” of the primary voice. There’s a lot of phasing going on.

Quite a bit of editing, overall, to attempt to take something very repetitive and make it a little less boring. Only partly-successful, but some nice noises here and there. Mixing pretty questionable—slamming it all into a limiter. (Amateur hour.) Maybe I will revisit it, probably not.

Oxi-Tetrachords sketch

This little sketch is a mere proof of concept, using OXI ONE as a gate sequencer with three different sets of gate patterns to drive Tetrachords. The second pattern shows up around 0:52. The third around 1:37.

There are three parts: two pretty simple, modulated E370 wavetable voices driven by Tetrachords arpeggiators, and one Tetrachord chord part that is playing through MIDI in a Bitwig synth.

Playing is pretty much just gradually opening the filters during the first pattern, closing them back down during the third, and triggering the pattern changes on the OXI ONE.

Almost no editing in the DAW other than setting levels, adding some reverb, and trimming off a few of the chord stabs at the tail end. In retrospect, I probably should have cropped off the first 18 seconds, too.

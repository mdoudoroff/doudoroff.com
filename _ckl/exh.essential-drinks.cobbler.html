cobbler

wine (less commonly, spirits), sugar, ice (generally shaken with, and garnished with fruit)

A key class of American mixed drink, apparently originating in New York, and one of the most popular from the 1840s–1900, particularly in the form of the Sherry Cobbler; also connected to the popularization of the drinking straw, due to its coldness

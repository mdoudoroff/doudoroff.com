Joseph Santini

1817–1874

One of the most famous bartenders of the mid-19th Century; invented the Brandy Crusta, a fancy drink

Santini owned and operated the “Jewel of the South” coffee house (bar) beginning in 1855, and also later “the Parlor”, both on Gravier Street. His “Jewel” was pretty much the fanciest bar in New Orleans in its day, and Santini was the first of New Orleans’ famous bartenders. Jerry Thomas may have visited the bar at some point, as at least three of Santini’s drinks—the Brandy Crusta, the Frozen Egg Nogg and a poussé café—are included in Thomas’ seminal 1862 book. Moreover, Santini is the only bartender Thomas mentions by name in his book, although he mispells it, and mis-identifies Santini as Spanish. Santini is buried in Saint Louis Cemetery Number 3, in New Orleans.

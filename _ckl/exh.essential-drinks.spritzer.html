spritzer

wine and sparkling water

The simple Spritzer—a bit like a wine sling or highball—began in Austro-Hungary as a way to lengthen and lighten the local wines for easier, more refreshing drinking. This practice remained popular in northern Italy after reunification. In the early twentieth century, variations incorporating vermouths and amari emerged. Two important examples of these variations are the Americano and Aperol Spritz.

(Oxford Companion to Spirits and Cocktails, p. 28–9)

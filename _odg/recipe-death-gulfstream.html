1939: Death in the Gulf Stream

“Jigger, Beaker and Flask”, Charles H. Baker, Jr.

This recipe is credited by Baker to his friend, the famously thirsty writer Ernest Hemingway. Paranoid about possibly being diabetic, Hemingway’s recipe omitted sugar, but even Baker felt it prudent to include a teaspoonful. On the other hand, without sugar it is close to being a bittered genever Rickey, which also sounds more than acceptable.

Death in the Gulf Stream

2 shots Old Duff Genever 40% abv
1 shot freshly squeezed lime juice
1 barspoon (5ml) rich sugar syrup (optional)
4 healthy dashes Angostura Aromatic Bitters

Build in a tall glass full of crushed ice
(Baker gave no further instructions but we advise swizzling the drink with a swizzle stick or barspoon until the outside of the glass frosts).
